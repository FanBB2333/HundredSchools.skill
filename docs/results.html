How Different Schools Affect Model Output Style

1. Mohist (Frugality): Output Compression and Structural Economy

The Mohist school's "jie yong" (frugality) principle instructs models to minimize token expenditure -- stripping prose, comments, usage examples, and alternative implementations. The effectiveness of this school correlates inversely with the model's tendency toward verbosity.

  • GLM-4.7 achieved the best compression (-73%) by aggressively eliminating entire sections. Its approach was "structural pruning" -- removing complete blocks of content.
  • GLM-5.1 (-49%) went beyond pruning to internalize the principle, adding __slots__ for memory efficiency and citing "jie yong" -- a philosophical behavior change, not just truncation.
  • Qwen 3.5 (-42%) explicitly acknowledged the Mohist approach in its opening line but retained both implementations. It interpreted "frugality" as "strip prose" rather than "minimize code."
  • MiniMax-M2.5 (-29%) achieved modest pruning but retained explanatory notes that violated the frugality principle.
  • Qwen 3.6 (-14%) showed the weakest compression despite adding __slots__ (structural optimization). Its baseline was already concise, limiting headroom, and it retained both implementations.

Key insight: Compression effectiveness depends on two factors: (1) how verbose the baseline is (more verbose = more room to compress), and (2) whether the model interprets frugality as "less prose" vs "less code" vs "optimize everything." The best results come from models that apply frugality holistically.

2. Logician (Formal Analysis): Analytical Depth and Logical Rigor

The Logician school tests whether models can shift from "quick answer" mode to formal logical analysis -- truth tables, fallacy naming, separation of interpretations, and self-reflection. This school reveals the most about a model's reasoning depth capacity.

  • GLM-5.1 (+380%) produced the most comprehensive analysis with all logician elements: 5-row truth table, named fallacy, "bai ma fei ma" (philosophical paradox reference), and explicit zi-xing.
  • Qwen 3.6 (+270%) was notably strong despite having the shortest baseline (406 bytes). It produced a "Name-to-Reality Mapping" section (direct kong ming ze shi adaptation), a formal truth table, and named the logical fallacy as "Implicit Grouping Assumption." The output demonstrated genuine framework internalization -- the model structured its entire analysis around the logician paradigm.
  • GLM-4.7 (+118%) produced structured analysis with explicit zi-xing and a nuanced verdict. Less expansive than GLM-5.1 but demonstrably skill-responsive.
  • Qwen 3.5 (+87%) produced a truth table and ambiguity analysis, but notably corrected its own baseline error: the baseline incorrectly stated "the function is correct" before backtracking. The logician mode eliminated this self-contradiction -- the school improved analytical accuracy.
  • MiniMax-M2.5 (-30%) fundamentally failed: output shrank instead of expanding, with no analytical elements added.

Key insight: The Logician school is the strongest discriminator of model capability. Models that can expand their analysis (add truth tables, name fallacies, perform self-reflection) demonstrate genuine reasoning depth. Models that compress instead of expanding reveal an inability to shift cognitive strategies based on instructions. Qwen 3.6's performance here is particularly interesting -- it produced fewer formal philosophy references than GLM-5.1 but structured its analysis more tightly around the logician framework.

3. Confucian (Mentor Tone): Communication Register and Persona

The Confucian school tests the most subtle model capability: persona transformation. Rather than changing output structure or volume, it requires the model to fundamentally shift how it communicates -- from reviewer to mentor, from critique to guidance, from judgment to encouragement.

  • Qwen 3.6 (+117%) achieved the deepest Confucian transformation. It explicitly framed its review as "written in the Confucian spirit of measured, constructive feedback," applied zheng ming with full explanation, offered 5 points of constructive guidance with reasoning, and closed with genuine relational warmth. This was not just labeling -- the entire communication register shifted.
  • GLM-5.1 (+81%) produced the classic benchmark result: full mentor persona with zheng ming and zhong yong references. The tone transformation was deep and consistent throughout.
  • MiniMax-M2.5 (+18%) and Qwen 3.5 (+12%) achieved only surface-level changes -- swapping a few words in the opening and adding a philosophy reference. The overall structure and tone remained reviewer-focused.
  • GLM-4.7 (+4%) showed the weakest transformation, confirming that models optimized for structural output manipulation struggle with nuanced persona shifts.

Key insight: Persona transformation is the hardest skill for models. It requires not just understanding instructions but embodying a different communication identity. The correlation between Logician and Confucian performance (Qwen 3.6 and GLM-5.1 excel at both) suggests that models with deeper "theory of mind" capabilities handle both analytical depth and persona shifts well. GLM-4.7's pattern (strong structural, weak persona) reveals a specific capability gap.

5. Philosophical Internalization vs Surface Compliance

A critical distinction emerged across models: internal vs external compliance.

  • Internalization (GLM-5.1, Qwen 3.6): The model not only follows instructions but adopts the philosophical framework as a reasoning paradigm. GLM-5.1 cited "jie yong" in code design choices (not just prose). Qwen 3.6 structured its entire logician analysis around "Name-to-Reality Mapping" -- it didn't add analysis features to a standard template; it rebuilt the template around the philosophy.
  • Surface compliance (Qwen 3.5, GLM-4.7 on Confucian): The model adds labels ("zheng ming," "Mohist approach") without changing its underlying reasoning or communication strategy. Qwen 3.5 explicitly said "I'll use the Mohist approach" but then delivered both implementations anyway.
  • Non-compliance (MiniMax-M2.5 on Logician): The model fails to interpret the instructions correctly, producing the opposite of the intended behavior.

Implication: The HundredSchools skill is most effective with models that have strong instruction-following capabilities and sufficient reasoning depth to apply philosophical frameworks to their cognitive process. Model capability is the limiting factor, not the skill design.
