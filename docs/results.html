HundredSchools: How Philosophical Schools Shape AI Output

Six schools of thought, one prompt, five models — tested via CLIppet on 2026-04-11

Prompt: "Write a Python function to validate an email address and explain your approach."

What does each school do to the output?

Each school of classical Chinese philosophy maps to a distinct control strategy for LLM output. The same prompt was sent to each model with and without each school activated, and the results measured in bytes as a proxy for output volume and structural change.

Mohist: Radical Compression

Activates "jie yong" (frugality). Strips prose, comments, examples, extras. Consistently the smallest output across all models: 25-62% of baseline. The most reliably activated school.

Average change-63%

Military: Strategic Expansion

Forces a <plan> block before action with dual-path (Zheng/Qi) strategy. Consistently the largest output: 1.4x-3.7x baseline. Adds resource assessment, fallback paths, and explicit scope.

Average change+113%

Logician: Analytical Depth

Adds truth tables, self-reflection (zi-xing), fallacy naming, and formal verification. Expands output 1.4x-2.6x with structured logical reasoning. Frames validation as falsifiable claims.

Average change+86%

Confucian: Formal Tone & Structure

Adds zheng ming, zhong yong references. Shifts tone to formal/mentoring. Maps validation stages to philosophical concepts. Output grows 1.1x-1.8x.

Average change+50%

Dao: Creative Exploration

Explores multiple paths, uses wu wei (non-action) principle. May produce alternative implementations or stop when returns diminish. The least reliably activated school -- one model failed entirely.

Average change+38%*

Legal: Strict Determinism

Enforces rule-based enumeration, RFC references, frozen dataclasses. Output varies: some models get terser (stricter), others expand with rules. Adds specification tables and deterministic guarantees.

Average change+21%

Key Insights: How Schools Shape Thinking

1. Schools control a cognitive spectrum from compression to expansion

The six schools form a clear spectrum of output behavior:

DirectionSchoolAverage ChangeCognitive Strategy
Compression Mohist-63%Strip everything non-essential
Legal+21% (varies)Constrain to rules (may compress or expand)
Expansion Dao+38%Explore multiple paths
Confucian+50%Add formality, structure, pedagogy
Logician+86%Add verification, self-reflection, formal proof
Military+113%Add planning, resource assessment, dual-path

2. Return type changes as a signal of deep internalization

One of the most telling indicators of how deeply a school affects model behavior is the return type of the generated function:

  • Mohist: Always bare bool -- maximum compression, no metadata
  • Confucian: Often tuple[bool, str] -- adds explanatory messages (propriety demands clarity)
  • Legal: frozen dataclass or tuple[bool, str] -- structured, immutable results
  • Military: bool with named regex groups -- separates concerns for inspection
  • Logician: dict with valid/reason/claims -- every claim must be auditable
  • Dao: dataclass or multiple functions -- explores different result shapes

The fact that models change data structures (not just prose) shows philosophical schools influence code architecture decisions, not just output formatting.

3. Mohist: The most reliable school

Mohist was the only school with 100% activation rate across all models. Every model successfully compressed output, stripped comments, and returned bare code. This suggests "do less" is the easiest cognitive instruction for LLMs to follow -- consistent with the observation that models default to verbose output and compression requires explicit instruction.

4. Dao: The hardest school to activate

Dao was the least reliably activated school. Qwen 3.5 completely failed (tried to invoke the skill literally). Other models produced valid output but with varying levels of Daoist framing. The "wu wei" principle of non-action is paradoxically the hardest instruction for an LLM to follow -- the model must know when to stop rather than just follow a format template.

5. Legal shows an interesting split: expansion vs compression

Unlike other schools which consistently push in one direction, Legal produced both expansion and compression depending on the model. Qwen 3.6 got shorter (-28%), interpreting "judge by law alone" as "strip everything except the strict implementation." GLM models got longer (+49%-98%), interpreting it as "enumerate all rules explicitly." Both interpretations are valid readings of Legalist philosophy -- this is the only school where the mapping is genuinely ambiguous.

6. Each school produces a unique cognitive signature

The schools don't just make output longer or shorter -- they produce qualitatively different cognitive strategies:

SchoolUnique Cognitive Signature
MohistElimination -- what can I remove?
DaoExploration -- what alternatives exist?
ConfucianPedagogy -- how should I communicate this?
LegalSpecification -- what are the exact rules?
MilitaryPlanning -- what's the strategy and fallback?
LogicianVerification -- how do I know this is correct?

This suggests the HundredSchools framework successfully maps ancient philosophical frameworks to distinct modern cognitive modes in AI systems.
